Current State Process (As-Is)

Overview

The support request intake process is largely manual. Support analysts are responsible for reviewing incoming emails, determining the issue category, searching for relevant knowledge articles, creating Jira tickets, and defining next actions.

This approach creates inconsistencies in ticket quality, longer response times, and increased operational effort.

Process Flow

1. User sends support request via email.
2. Support analyst opens and reads the email.
3. Analyst determines the issue category manually.
4. Analyst assesses business impact and priority.
5. Analyst searches internal documentation or knowledge bases.
6. Analyst identifies a possible resolution approach.
7. Analyst creates a Jira ticket manually.
8. Analyst copies email information into Jira fields.
9. Analyst assigns the ticket to an appropriate support team.
10. Analyst documents troubleshooting steps.
11. Analyst communicates with the requester.

Challenges

* Manual ticket classification.
* Inconsistent prioritization.
* Knowledge articles are searched manually.
* Ticket quality varies by analyst experience.
* Increased response times.
* Repetitive administrative work.
* Limited scalability.

Business Impact

The manual process consumes valuable support resources and increases the time required to move incidents from intake to resolution.
